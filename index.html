Lata Mangeshkar

Lata Mangeshkar (28 September 1929 - 6 February 2022) was an Indian singer. Her contribution to the Indian music industry in a career spanning eight decades gained her honorific titles such as the "Queen of Melody", "Nightingale of India", and "Voice of the Millennium".

Early life and education

  • 1) Lata Mangeshkar was born the daughter of Dinanath Mangeshkar, the owner of a theater company and a reputed classical singer in his own right.
  • 2) His father started giving Lata singing lessons from the age of five, and she also studied with renowned singers Aman Ali Khan Sahib and Amanat Khan.
  • 3) Lata Mangeshkar never went to school but has six doctorate degrees. After her father passed away when was only 13 years, she began taking care of the family as she was the eldest daughter.

Personal life

  • 1) The Legendary singer and Bharat Ratana Awardee chose to stay unmarried all her life. While Mangeshkar mostly steered clear from answering questions about her personal life, on a rare occasion the singer shared her thoughts on love, marriage and children and their gravity in her life.
  • 2) Her father, Pandit Deenanath Mangeshkar, was a classical singer and theatre actor and her mother, Shevanti, a Gujarati woman, was the second wife of her father. She had a younger brother Hridaynath Mangeshkar and 3 younger sisters, Meena Khadikar, Asha Bhosle, and Usha Mangeshkar.

Illness and death

  • 1) LATA MANGESHKAR'S LAST MESSAGE - "Yada yada hi dharmasya glanirbhavati bharata. Abhythanamadharmasya tadatmanam srijamyaham. Paritranaya sadhunang vinashay cha dushkritam. Dharmasangsthapanarthay sambhabami yuge yuge."
  • 2) The 92-year-old singer died at a Mumbai hospital on Sunday morning due to multiple organ failure
